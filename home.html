Thangka is a kind of painting art that emerged in Songtsen Gampo's period, namely scroll painting framed with colored satin, with distinctive Tibetan characteristics, strong religious colors and unique artistic style.

Each procedure should follow the Tibetan Buddhist rituals, and the 'Sutra on the Measurements of the Image' is a clear regulation on the content, coloring and size of the thang-ga, and the painter must make the image for the Buddhas according to the Sutra on the Measurements without any overstepping. Thang-ga is a unique form of painting art in Tibetan culture, and is regarded as a treasure of Chinese national art.




Thangka is known as the Tibetan 'encyclopedia', which covers a wide range of subjects, including religious paintings, biographies, historical paintings, genre paintings reflecting life customs, and scientific paintings reflecting astronomical calendar, Tibetan medicine, human anatomy, etc. On May 24, 2021, the Khammensa Thangka declared by Changdu City of Tibet Autonomous Region was approved by the State Council to be included in the extended list of representative items of national intangible cultural heritage (heritage number: VII-14).

Origin


There are four kinds of Tangka origin that are now proven reliable:

Bon origin theory, Han origin theory,

Songtsen Gampo nosebleed origin theory,

and Indian cloth painting origin theory.

Core

The norm of Thangka in ancient times, as a 'moving temple' of nomads, provided them with the function of praying and worshiping at any time.


It could also be regarded as a handbook for Tibetan people to record political, economic, cultural and other aspects of life.


Thangka has been the cultural treasure of the Chinese nation and the embodiment of people's spiritual belief in the material world, and the door to the spiritual world.
